About Me

I am a Master's student at Northeastern University majoring in Bioinformatics, with a strong focus on bioinformatics and computational biology.


I spent 8 years in Seoul, South Korea, where I earned my undergraduate degree in Life Science and Biotechnology at Yonsei University. After gaining three years of industry experience, I decided to move to the U.S. to further my studies, deepen my expertise, and become skilled with computational tools.


With a strong foundation in both wet-lab techniques and programming, I specialize in connecting biological questions with data-driven solutions. I have a keen interest in cancer biology, especially oncology treatments, drug discovery and drug development. I unravel the story of data and make sense of the mystery by using computational methods—transforming raw biological information into meaningful insights that can drive research forward.


To me, bioinformatics is more than just a career path—it's a powerful tool to protect the people I care about and and a means to contribute to the future of precision medicine.
